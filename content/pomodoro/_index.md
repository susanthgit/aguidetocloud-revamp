  - question: "What is the Pomodoro Technique?"
    answer: "The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. You work in focused intervals (traditionally 25 minutes) called 'pomodoros', separated by short breaks (5 minutes). After four pomodoros, you take a longer break (15–30 minutes). This rhythm helps maintain deep focus while preventing burnout."
  - question: "Why are the focus sessions 25 minutes by default?"
    answer: "Research suggests 25 minutes is long enough to make meaningful progress on a task, but short enough to maintain intense concentration. However, everyone is different — our timer lets you customise sessions from 1 to 90 minutes. Many developers prefer 50-minute sessions, while students often find 25 minutes ideal."
  - question: "Does the Pomodoro Technique work for studying?"
    answer: "Absolutely. The technique is especially effective for studying because it combats procrastination, creates urgency, and forces regular review breaks where your brain consolidates information. Many students report significantly better retention when using timed study sessions with breaks."
  - question: "Is my data saved? Is it private?"

If you clear your browser data, your stats will be reset. Standard anonymous page analytics (Google Analytics) apply to all pages on the site, but no tool-specific data — passwords, images, palettes, etc. — is ever collected or transmitted."
