faq:
  - question: "Do I need Microsoft 365 E3 or E5 to buy Copilot?"
    answer: "You need a qualifying base licence. M365 E3, M365 E5, M365 Business Standard, M365 Business Premium, Office 365 E3/E5, and a few others qualify. F1/F3 frontline plans currently do not include eligibility for the M365 Copilot add-on."
  - question: "Is Copilot Chat the same as M365 Copilot?"
    answer: "No. Copilot Chat is the free, web-grounded chat experience with no organisational data access. M365 Copilot is the paid $30/user/month add-on that grounds answers in your tenant's data and works inside Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, Teams, and more."
  - question: "Can I trial Copilot before committing?"
    answer: "Microsoft offers paid pilots through partners and account teams (typically 300+ users). There's no self-serve free trial of M365 Copilot — but Copilot Chat is free and gives a feel for the chat experience without tenant grounding."
  - question: "What's the minimum number of Copilot licences I can buy?"
    answer: "Microsoft removed the original 300-seat minimum. Most customers can buy as few as one licence today via their CSP or direct, depending on agreement type. Check with your account team for current minimums."
