  - question: "Are these questions for beginners or experienced M365 admins?"
    answer: "Mixed — that's the point. The list opens with fundamentals (Exchange recipient types, SharePoint site templates, Teams licensing) for first-time M365 candidates, then progresses to harder scenarios (Conditional Access design, hybrid Entra Connect, Defender XDR investigation flows) for senior admins. Experienced folks: skip to the second half. Beginners: don't get demoralised at the senior questions — interviewers usually skip them for entry-level roles."
  - question: "M365 vs Azure interview prep — do they overlap?"
    answer: "They share identity (Entra ID) and security (Defender, Purview) heavily but diverge on workloads. Azure interviews lean on infrastructure (VNets, storage, compute, IaC), M365 interviews lean on productivity apps (Exchange, SharePoint, Teams) and end-user identity and compliance. If you're interviewing for a hybrid role (M365 admin in an Azure-heavy enterprise), prep both — our [100 Azure Interview Questions](/interview-prep/100-azure-interview-questions-and-answers-beginner-to-advanced-guide-for-landing/) post covers the Azure side."
  - question: "How do I prep for an M365 interview if I've only done end-user work?"
    answer: "Honest answer — you'll struggle without 4 to 6 weeks of admin-portal time. End-user familiarity is helpful but interviewers test admin concepts (licensing, role assignments, conditional access policies, retention labels). Get a [free M365 developer tenant](https://developer.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-365/dev-program) (full E5 sandbox), break things, fix them. Pair that with the [MS-900](/cert-tracker/ms-900/) fundamentals cert to ground your vocabulary. Then run through these interview questions out loud."
