## About the DCACI Exam

> Implement and operate a Cisco ACI fabric end to end — the fabric, the policy model, and everywhere

The 300-620 DCACI (Implementing Cisco Application Centric Infrastructure, exam topics **v1.2**) is a **CCNP concentration exam** — pair it with the DCCOR 350-601 core for **CCNP Data Center**. Passing also earns the **Cisco Certified Specialist - Data Center ACI Implementation** credential. It validates your ability to implement and operate a **Cisco ACI** fabric: the **fabric infrastructure** (the spine/leaf topology, the **APIC** cluster and Virtual APIC, the object model / Management Information Tree and APIC REST, faults and health scores, fabric discovery, access and fabric policies, and the logical constructs — tenants, VRFs, bridge domains, EPGs, **ESGs**, and contracts with `vzAny` and preferred group); **packet forwarding** (endpoint learning, the **COOP** mapping database, and bridge-domain settings such as unicast routing and hardware-proxy versus flood); **external connectivity** (EPG static path bindings, STP interop, **MCP**, L2Out, and **L3Out** with OSPF/BGP/EIGRP/static routing and external EPGs); **integrations** (VMware vCenter DVS and **Nutanix** VMM domains, resolution and deployment immediacy, and L4-7 **service graphs** with **PBR**); **ACI management** (in-band and out-of-band access, syslog/SNMP and **Nexus Dashboard Insights**, configuration snapshots, AAA/RBAC, and upgrades); and **ACI Anywhere** (Multi-Pod, Multi-Site with the Nexus Dashboard Orchestrator, and Remote Leaf). Original practice questions. Not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sourced from Cisco Systems certification exams.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

DCACI is designed for **data center network engineers, ACI fabric administrators, and solutions architects who design, implement, and operate Cisco ACI fabrics** built on the APIC and Nexus 9000 spine/leaf switches. It suits candidates pursuing CCNP Data Center. Cisco recommends 3-5 years of data center networking experience plus hands-on time with ACI — the APIC GUI and REST API, the policy model (tenants, VRFs, bridge domains, EPGs, contracts), external connectivity (L2Out/L3Out), VMM and L4-7 service integrations, fabric management, and the ACI Anywhere designs (Multi-Pod, Multi-Site, Remote Leaf). Note that the retired 300-630 DCACIA (Advanced ACI Implementation) content — including Remote Leaf — was consolidated into 300-620 in the v1.2 update.

**Typical study time:** 6-10 weeks of focused study

## Exam Domains & Weights

The DCACI exam covers **6 domains**. Focus your study time based on the weights below — higher-weighted domains have more exam questions.

| Domain | Weight | Practice Qs |

| ACI Fabric Infrastructure | 20% | 50 |

| ACI Anywhere | 10% | 25 |
| **Total** | **100%** | **250** |

> 💡 **Study tip:** The three 20% domains — **ACI Fabric Infrastructure**, **External Network Connectivity**, and **ACI Management** — together make up **60% of the exam**, so spend most of your time there. Be fluent in the access-policy chain (switch profile → interface profile → policy group → AEP → domain → VLAN pool), the difference between EPGs and **ESGs**, an **L3Out** with OSPF or BGP and its external EPG subnet scopes, VMM **resolution vs deployment immediacy**, a service graph with **PBR**, and in-band vs out-of-band management with contracts. In **ACI Anywhere** (10%), know that Multi-Pod uses one APIC cluster over an IPN while Multi-Site uses separate fabrics orchestrated by the **Nexus Dashboard Orchestrator (NDO)**, and how Remote Leaf differs from both.

## Cisco Certification Path

DCACI is a **concentration** exam. For **CCNP Data Center**, pass the **DCCOR 350-601** core plus one concentration — **300-620 DCACI** (ACI implementation) is one of four concentration choices alongside DCID 300-610, DCIT 300-615, and DCNAUTO 300-635. Cisco's track runs: Associate (CCNA) → Professional (CCNP) → Expert (CCIE).

## Study Tips

1. **Start with the three 20% domains** — ACI Fabric Infrastructure, External Network Connectivity, and ACI Management are the biggest slices
2. **Know the policy model cold** — tenants, VRFs, bridge domains, application profiles, EPGs, ESGs, and contracts (with subjects, filters, `vzAny`, and preferred group) are the language of every ACI question
3. **Use our practice exam** — try the 20 free questions first to gauge your readiness
4. **Review explanations** — don't just check if you got it right; read why each answer is correct
5. **Master the tricky knobs** — bridge-domain **Hardware Proxy vs Flood** and ARP flooding, VMM **Pre-Provision** resolution vs **On-Demand** deployment, and L3Out subnet scopes are classic exam traps
6. **Use current names** — Nexus Dashboard Insights (NDI) and Nexus Dashboard Orchestrator / NDO (not MSO) are the current products
7. **Simulate exam conditions** — use the timed exam mode to practice under pressure
