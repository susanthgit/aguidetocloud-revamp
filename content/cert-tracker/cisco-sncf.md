## About the SNCF Exam

> Master Cisco Secure Firewall — deployment, FMC policy configuration, Snort, troubleshooting, and integrations

The 300-710 SNCF (Securing Networks with Cisco Firewalls, formerly Securing Networks with Cisco Firepower) is a CCNP Security concentration exam. It validates your ability to deploy, configure, manage, and troubleshoot Cisco Secure Firewall Threat Defense (FTD) and the Secure Firewall Management Center (FMC): routed and transparent deployment modes, NGIPS, high availability and clustering, access control and intrusion and malware and decryption policies, Snort, NAT and VPN, and integrations with Secure Endpoint, Threat Intelligence Director, Cisco XDR, ISE pxGrid, and Splunk. Passing SNCF earns the Cisco Certified Specialist - Network Security Firepower badge and, with the SCOR 350-701 core, the CCNP Security certification. Original practice questions. Not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sourced from Cisco Systems certification exams.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

SNCF is designed for **security engineers, network security administrators, and architects who deploy and operate Cisco Secure Firewall**. Cisco recommends 3-5 years of security-solution implementation experience; hands-on time with FMC, FTD, and Snort policies helps a lot.

**Typical study time:** 6-10 weeks of focused study

## Exam Domains & Weights

The SNCF exam covers **4 domains**. Focus your study time based on the weights below — higher-weighted domains have more exam questions.

| Domain | Weight | Practice Qs |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Deployment | 30% | 74 |
| Configuration | 30% | 74 |
| Management and Troubleshooting | 25% | 62 |
| Integration | 15% | 40 |
| **Total** | **100%** | **250** |

> 💡 **Study tip:** **Deployment** and **Configuration** each carry 30% — together that is 60% of the exam, so start there. Make sure you can walk the full traffic path through the policy stack in order — **Security Intelligence**, then the **prefilter policy**, then the **access control policy**, then the **intrusion / malware / file** inspection — because rule evaluation order is tested across multiple domains.

## Cisco Certification Path

Cisco certs follow: Entry (CCT) → Associate (CCNA) → Professional (CCNP) → Expert (CCIE). CCNP Security requires the **SCOR 350-701** core exam plus **one concentration** — SNCF 300-710 is the Secure Firewall (Firepower) concentration.
