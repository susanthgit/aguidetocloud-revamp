## About the CGOA Exam

> Prove you understand the GitOps operating model — vendor-neutral, tool-agnostic

Master GitOps end to end — the core terminology (desired state, drift, reconciliation, the state store, feedback loops, and rollback), the four OpenGitOps principles (declarative; versioned and immutable; pulled automatically; continuously reconciled), the practices that surround GitOps (Configuration as Code, Infrastructure as Code, DevOps/DevSecOps, and CI/CD), the deployment, progressive-delivery, and architecture patterns, and the tool-agnostic tooling landscape (manifest packaging, state stores, and reconciliation engines such as Argo CD and Flux). Original practice questions covering all 5 official CGOA domains. Not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sourced from CNCF or Linux Foundation certification exams.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

The CGOA is designed for **platform engineers, DevOps engineers, SREs, release engineers, and engineering leads** who are adopting or evaluating GitOps. There are no formal prerequisites; a working familiarity with Git, CI/CD, and Kubernetes-style declarative configuration is helpful but not required. Because the exam is tool-agnostic, it tests your understanding of the GitOps model itself rather than any single product.

**Typical study time:** 2-4 weeks of focused study

## Exam Domains & Weights

The CGOA exam covers **5 domains**. Focus your study time based on the weights below — higher-weighted domains have more exam questions.

| Domain | Weight | Practice Qs |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| GitOps Terminology | 20% | 50 |
| GitOps Principles | 30% | 75 |
| Related Practices | 16% | 40 |
| GitOps Patterns | 20% | 50 |
| Tooling | 14% | 35 |
| **Total** | **100%** | **250** |

> 💡 **Study tip:** **GitOps Principles** (30%) is the single heaviest domain — memorize the four OpenGitOps principles (declarative; versioned and immutable; pulled automatically; continuously reconciled) and be able to judge whether a described system actually satisfies all four. A CI pipeline that runs kubectl apply on merge is automation, but it is not GitOps (it is push-based and not continuously reconciled). Terminology (20%) and Patterns (20%) are the next priorities.
