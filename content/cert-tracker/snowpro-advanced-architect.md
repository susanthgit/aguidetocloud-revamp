## About the SnowPro Advanced: Architect Exam

> Master the Snowflake SnowPro Advanced: Architect certification (ARA-C01) — account and security architecture, the Snowflake AI Data Cloud architecture and business continuity, data engineering pipeline design, and performance and cost optimization.

The complete practice exam for the Snowflake SnowPro Advanced: Architect certification (ARA-C01). This advanced, architect-level exam goes deeper than SnowPro Core — it tests how you design enterprise-scale Snowflake solutions and choose the right capability for each trade-off. Covers accounts and security architecture (organization and account strategy, the ACCOUNTADMIN / SECURITYADMIN / USERADMIN role hierarchy, RBAC / DAC / UBAC and future grants, managed access schemas, SSO / SCIM / OAuth / key-pair / MFA, network policies and rules, PrivateLink / Private Link / Private Service Connect, dynamic data masking and row access policies, object tagging and tag-based masking, secure views, Tri-Secret Secure encryption, ACCESS_HISTORY auditing, and replication of security objects), the Snowflake architecture (the three-layer design, virtual and multi-cluster warehouses, micro-partitions, Time Travel and Fail-safe, multi-account and organization strategy, replication groups and failover groups, Snowgrid cross-region and cross-cloud business continuity, Client Redirect, Secure Data Sharing, listings and the Marketplace, Apache Iceberg tables and external volumes, Kafka / Spark / dbt integrations, Snowpark and Streamlit), data engineering (COPY INTO and stages on S3 / Azure / GCS, file formats, Snowpipe and Snowpipe Streaming, streams and tasks and task graphs, dynamic tables, semi-structured VARIANT / FLATTEN, MERGE and zero-copy cloning, Snowpark DataFrames, UDFs and stored procedures, and source-to-curated layering), and performance optimization (warehouse sizing and multi-cluster scaling, the Query Acceleration Service and Search Optimization Service, clustering keys and micro-partition pruning, materialized views, caching, Query Profile and EXPLAIN, ACCOUNT_USAGE monitoring, resource monitors, and cost optimization) — every question a real-world architecture scenario with full explanations.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

The SnowPro Advanced: Architect certification is designed for **senior data architects, principal engineers, platform leads, and solution architects** who design and govern enterprise-scale workloads on the Snowflake AI Data Cloud. It validates advanced design judgment across security architecture, business continuity, pipeline design, and performance and cost optimization.

**Prerequisites:** SnowPro Core Certified

**Typical study time:** 6-10 weeks of focused study (plus hands-on Snowflake architecture experience)

## Exam Domains & Weights

The SnowPro Advanced: Architect exam covers **4 domains**. Focus your study time based on the weights below — higher-weighted domains have more exam questions.

| Domain | Weight | Practice Qs |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Snowflake Architecture | 30% | 75 |
| Accounts and Security | 25% | 63 |
| Data Engineering | 25% | 62 |
| Performance Optimization | 20% | 50 |
| **Total** | **100%** | **250** |

> 💡 **Study tip:** Snowflake Architecture (30%) is the heaviest domain, so be ready to reason about the three-layer design, multi-cluster warehouses, and — above all — business continuity: know exactly when to use a **replication group** versus a **failover group**, how Snowgrid replicates across regions and clouds, and how Client Redirect keeps clients connected through a failover. Accounts and Security (25%) rewards deep RBAC design (future grants, managed access schemas, separation of duties) plus masking, row access, and Tri-Secret Secure. Data Engineering (25%) is about choosing the right pattern — Snowpipe vs Snowpipe Streaming, dynamic tables vs streams-and-tasks. Performance Optimization (20%) is decision-making: clustering vs Search Optimization vs Query Acceleration, and reading Query Profile to spot spilling and poor pruning.
