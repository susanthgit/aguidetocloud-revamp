## About the Nutanix NCP-US Exam

> Master the Nutanix Certified Professional - Unified Storage (NCP-US 6.10) exam — deploying, configuring, monitoring, and troubleshooting Nutanix Files, Objects, and Volumes, using File Analytics and Data Lens for data security, and protecting file, object, and block data the Nutanix-native way.

The complete practice exam for the Nutanix Certified Professional - Unified Storage (NCP-US 6.10) certification, built on current Nutanix Unified Storage running on AOS. Covers deploying and upgrading Nutanix Unified Storage (enabling and deploying a scale-out Nutanix Files server with a minimum of three File Server VMs, Active Directory and DNS integration, deploying Nutanix Objects on the microservices platform with an S3 endpoint, Life Cycle Manager upgrades and maintenance for Files and Objects, and choosing and sizing the right product for a workload); configuring and utilizing Nutanix Unified Storage (distributed home shares versus standard general shares, SMB and NFS options, quotas, access-based enumeration, and self-service restore for Files; Volume Groups, virtual disks, the Data Services IP, CHAP authentication, and multipathing for Volumes; buckets, S3 access keys, versioning, WORM Object Lock, lifecycle rules, and bucket replication for Objects; and determining the right way to ensure data availability and recoverability); analyzing and monitoring Nutanix Unified Storage (File Analytics for on-premises Files auditing, anomaly and ransomware detection, and capacity trends; monitoring performance and usage in Prism; and Data Lens for multi-cluster Files-and-Objects ransomware protection, WORM, lifecycle tiering, and SOC integration); and troubleshooting Nutanix Unified Storage (Files share access, permissions, and authentication; Objects S3 endpoint, DNS, certificate, and access-key errors; Volumes iSCSI connectivity and CHAP; data-management processes; and failed Life Cycle Manager upgrades) — every question an original real-world Nutanix storage scenario with full explanations.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

The NCP-US certification is designed for **storage administrators, infrastructure and virtualization engineers, backup and DR engineers, and IT architects** who deliver file, object, and block storage on Nutanix. It validates practical, day-to-day skills across Nutanix Files, Objects, and Volumes — from deployment and configuration through analytics, data protection, and troubleshooting on the Nutanix Cloud Platform.

**Prerequisites:** None required (Nutanix recommends ~1-2 years of general IT experience with 6-12 months of data-storage experience and the Nutanix Unified Storage Administration course)

**Typical study time:** 4-8 weeks of focused study

> 💡 **Study tip:** Keep the three products distinct — Nutanix Files (SMB/NFS shares, a minimum of three FSVMs, distributed home shares versus standard shares), Nutanix Objects (S3-compatible buckets, access keys, versioning, WORM Object Lock, lifecycle rules), and Nutanix Volumes (iSCSI Volume Groups reached through the Data Services IP with CHAP). Know which analytics tool fits — File Analytics is the embedded, Files-only, on-premises audit module, while Data Lens is the broader multi-cluster platform covering Files and Objects with cloud-connected ransomware protection, WORM, tiering, and SOC integration. For protection, match the mechanism to the product: Smart DR for Files, bucket replication for Objects, and Volume Group snapshots for Volumes — not AOS Protection Domains and not NDB Time Machine.

## Nutanix Certification Path

The **NCP-US (Unified Storage)** is a Nutanix professional specialisation focused on the Nutanix data-services layer — Files, Objects, and Volumes. It pairs well with the core **NCP-MCI (Multicloud Infrastructure)** credential: take NCP-MCI to validate broad cluster-operations skills, then add NCP-US to prove depth in unified storage. Both sit under the wider Nutanix Certified Professional and Master (NCM) tracks.

## Study Tips

1. **Separate the three storage products** — be fluent in when Nutanix Files (file shares), Objects (S3 buckets), or Volumes (iSCSI block) is the right answer for a workload
2. **Use our practice exam** — try the 20 free questions first to gauge your readiness
3. **Get hands-on** — deploy Files, create an object store and a bucket, and attach a Volume Group over iSCSI to internalise the constructs
