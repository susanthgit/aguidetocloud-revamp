## About the SnowPro Advanced: Security Engineer Exam

> Master the Snowflake SnowPro Advanced: Security Engineer certification (SEA-C01) — access control and identity, data protection and governance, auditing and compliance, threat response and forensics, and securing AI/ML and applications.

The complete practice exam for the Snowflake SnowPro Advanced: Security Engineer certification (SEA-C01). This advanced, role-based exam tests how you secure and govern the Snowflake AI Data Cloud. Covers access control and identity management (RBAC automation and hierarchies, functional vs access roles, SCIM provisioning, Snowflake-managed and external MFA, SSO with SAML and OAuth, key-pair and Programmatic Access Token authentication, authentication and session policies, network rules and network policies, AWS PrivateLink/Azure Private Link/GCP Private Service Connect, external access integrations and secrets), data protection, privacy and governance (Tri-Secret Secure, Dynamic Data Masking and tag-based masking, External Tokenization, projection, row-access, aggregation and differential-privacy policies, Data Clean Rooms, Secure Data Sharing, exfiltration controls, Time Travel and Fail-safe, object tagging and classification, secure replication and failover), auditing, monitoring and compliance (QUERY_HISTORY/ACCESS_HISTORY/LOGIN_HISTORY, ACCOUNT_USAGE, Snowflake Trail, Trust Center, alerts via tasks and streams, GDPR/HIPAA/CCPA/PCI DSS), threats, risk assessment, incident response and forensics (threat modeling, Horizon Catalog risk, containment and eradication, chain-of-custody forensics), and securing AI/ML and applications (Snowpark Container Services, Cortex LLM content moderation, secure Native Apps) — every question a real-world security scenario with full explanations.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

The SnowPro Advanced: Security Engineer certification is designed for **security engineers, platform administrators, and governance leads** who protect data and access on the Snowflake AI Data Cloud. It validates advanced, hands-on skill across access control, data protection, auditing, threat response, and securing modern AI/ML and application workloads. Candidates typically have 2+ years of hands-on Snowflake plus a security background.

**Prerequisites:** SnowPro Core Certified

**Typical study time:** 6-10 weeks of focused study (plus hands-on RBAC, masking, network policy, and audit experience)

## Exam Domains & Weights

The SnowPro Advanced: Security Engineer exam covers **5 domains**. Focus your study time based on the weights below — higher-weighted domains have more exam questions.

| Domain | Weight | Practice Qs |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Data Protection, Data Privacy, and Data Governance | 30% | 75 |
| Access Control and Identity Management | 22% | 55 |
| Auditing, Monitoring, and Compliance | 18% | 45 |
| Threats, Risk Assessment, Incident Response, and Forensics | 18% | 45 |
| Securing Snowflake Services and Features for AI/ML and Applications | 12% | 30 |
| **Total** | **100%** | **250** |

> 💡 **Study tip:** Data Protection (30%) is the heaviest domain — know Dynamic Data Masking and tag-based masking, External Tokenization, row-access, aggregation and differential-privacy policies, Tri-Secret Secure, Data Clean Rooms, exfiltration controls, Time Travel vs Fail-safe, classification, and secure replication and failover. Access Control (22%) is RBAC and least-privilege role design, SCIM, MFA, SSO (SAML/OAuth), key-pair and PAT auth, network policies, and external access integrations. Auditing and Compliance (18%) covers QUERY/ACCESS/LOGIN_HISTORY, ACCOUNT_USAGE, Trust Center, and GDPR/HIPAA/CCPA/PCI DSS. Threats and Incident Response (18%) is threat modeling, Horizon Catalog risk, containment, and forensics. Securing AI/ML and Apps (12%) is Snowpark Container Services, Cortex moderation, and secure Native Apps.

## Study Tips

1. **Lead with data protection** — the heaviest domain rewards knowing masking, tokenization, row-access and aggregation policies, Tri-Secret Secure, and secure replication cold
2. **Use our practice exam** — try the 20 free questions first to gauge your readiness for the advanced level
3. **Get hands-on in a trial account** — build RBAC hierarchies, masking and network policies, configure SSO, and read QUERY/ACCESS/LOGIN_HISTORY in a real Snowflake account
4. **Think in trade-offs** — the security exam is about *choosing* (managed vs external MFA; key-pair vs PAT; Time Travel vs Fail-safe for recovery); read every explanation for the why
5. **Check the official page** — [official exam details](https://learn.snowflake.com/en/certifications/snowpro-advanced-securityengineer/) always have the latest objectives
