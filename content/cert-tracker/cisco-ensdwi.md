## About the ENSDWI Exam

> Master Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN — architecture, controllers, WAN Edge deployment, policies, security, and operations

The 300-415 ENSDWI (Implementing Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Solutions) is a CCNP Enterprise concentration exam. It validates your ability to deploy and operate the Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN (Viptela-based) fabric: the orchestration, management, control, and data planes (SD-WAN Validator/vBond, SD-WAN Manager/vManage, SD-WAN Controller/vSmart, OMP, TLOCs), WAN Edge onboarding, centralized and localized policies, end-to-end segmentation, on-box and cloud security, QoS, Cloud OnRamp, and day-2 operations. Passing ENSDWI earns the Cisco Certified Specialist - Enterprise SD-WAN Implementation badge and, with ENCOR 350-401, the CCNP Enterprise certification. Original practice questions. Not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sourced from Cisco Systems certification exams.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

ENSDWI is designed for **network engineers and architects who design, deploy, and operate Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN**. Cisco recommends 3-5 years of enterprise networking experience; hands-on time with SD-WAN Manager, templates, and policies helps a lot.

**Typical study time:** 6-10 weeks of focused study

## Exam Domains & Weights

The ENSDWI exam covers **6 domains**. Focus your study time based on the weights below — higher-weighted domains have more exam questions.

| Domain | Weight | Practice Qs |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Architecture | 20% | 50 |
| Controller Deployment | 15% | 38 |
| Router Deployment | 20% | 50 |
| Policies | 20% | 50 |
| Security and Quality of Service | 15% | 37 |
| Management and Operations | 10% | 25 |
| **Total** | **100%** | **250** |

> 💡 **Study tip:** **Architecture**, **Router Deployment**, and **Policies** each carry 20% — they are the core of the exam, so start there. Make sure you can clearly separate **centralized control policy** (shapes OMP route advertisement on the Controller) from **centralized data policy** (acts on traffic at the WAN Edge) — that distinction shows up across multiple domains.
