## About the DCNAUTO Exam

> Automate the Cisco data center end to end — the fabric, the controller, and with AI

The 300-635 DCNAUTO (Automating Cisco Data Center Networking Solutions, exam topics **v2.0**) is a **CCNP concentration exam** — pair it with the DCCOR 350-601 core for **CCNP Data Center**, or with the AUTOCOR 350-901 core for **CCNP Automation**. Passing also earns the **Cisco Certified Automation Specialist - Data Center Automation and Programmability** credential. It validates your ability to automate Cisco data center networking: the YANG/NETCONF/gNMI/gRPC/gNOI foundation and the **ACI** network-centric object model (EPGs, bridge domains, contracts, VRFs); **Infrastructure as Code** with Jinja2, Ansible (the `cisco.nxos`, `cisco.aci`, `cisco.dcnm`, and `cisco.mso` collections) and Terraform (the CiscoDevNet `aci`, `nxos`, and `ndfc` providers) under GitOps; **network element programmability** with Python `ncclient`, Day-0 **POAP**, on-box NX-OS Bash/Python and the Guest Shell, **Nexus Dashboard**/**NDFC** templates, and **NX-API**; **operations** with Cisco Modeling Labs, pyATS, model-driven telemetry (gNMI/gRPC), a NetBox source of truth, and containerized packet-flow troubleshooting on Linux; and the new **AI in Automation** domain. Original practice questions. Not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sourced from Cisco Systems certification exams.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

DCNAUTO is designed for **data center network engineers, NetDevOps and automation engineers, and platform/SRE staff who automate Cisco data center fabrics** — ACI and NX-OS/Nexus estates managed through controllers and code. It suits candidates pursuing CCNP Data Center or CCNP Automation. Cisco recommends 3-5 years of data center networking experience plus hands-on time with Python, Ansible, Terraform, Jinja2, and Cisco data center programmability (NETCONF/RESTCONF/YANG, ACI/APIC, NX-API, Nexus Dashboard/NDFC, model-driven telemetry, pyATS).

**Typical study time:** 8-12 weeks of focused study

## Exam Domains & Weights

The DCNAUTO exam covers **5 domains**. Focus your study time based on the weights below — higher-weighted domains have more exam questions.

| Domain | Weight | Practice Qs |

> 💡 **Study tip:** The three 25% domains — **Infrastructure as Code**, **Network Element Programmability**, and **Operations** — together make up **75% of the exam**, so spend most of your time there. Be fluent in an Ansible playbook with a controller collection (`cisco.aci`, `cisco.dcnm`) and a device collection (`cisco.nxos`), a Terraform plan with the CiscoDevNet `aci`/`nxos`/`ndfc` providers, a Python `ncclient` NETCONF session against NX-OS, Day-0 **POAP**, on-box Guest Shell/Python, an **NDFC** template, and a gNMI/gRPC model-driven-telemetry subscription. The new **AI in Automation** domain (10%) is fresh in v2.0 — know the security implications of AI in automation, how agentic AI (and MCP) connects to platforms and controllers, and how generative AI accelerates prototyping.

## Cisco Certification Path

DCNAUTO is a **concentration** exam. For **CCNP Data Center**, pass the **DCCOR 350-601** core plus one concentration (DCNAUTO is the automation/programmability choice). For **CCNP Automation**, pass the **AUTOCOR 350-901** core plus one concentration — **300-635 DCNAUTO** (Data Center) or **300-435 ENAUTO** (Enterprise). Cisco's track runs: Associate → Professional (CCNP) → Expert (CCIE).

## Study Tips

1. **Start with the three 25% domains** — Infrastructure as Code, Network Element Programmability, and Operations are three-quarters of the exam
2. **Know each tool by hand** — a `cisco.nxos`/`cisco.aci`/`cisco.dcnm` Ansible playbook, a CiscoDevNet Terraform plan, a Python `ncclient` NETCONF session, an NX-API call, a POAP flow, and an NDFC template are tested as applied skills, not trivia
3. **Use our practice exam** — try the 20 free questions first to gauge your readiness
4. **Review explanations** — don't just check if you got it right; read why each answer is correct
5. **Configure telemetry cold** — `feature telemetry`, sensor-group, destination-group, subscription, and the gNMI dial-in vs gRPC dial-out distinction come up in Operations
6. **Use current names** — Nexus Dashboard Fabric Controller / NDFC (not DCNM), Nexus Dashboard Orchestrator / NDO (not MSO), Cisco Modeling Labs / CML (not VIRL)
7. **Simulate exam conditions** — use the timed exam mode to practice under pressure
