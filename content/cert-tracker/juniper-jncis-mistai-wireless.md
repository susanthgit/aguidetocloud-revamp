## About the JNCIS-MistAI-Wireless Exam

> Specialist Juniper Mist wireless - RF, WLAN deployment, WxLAN, WIDS/WIPS, Wireless Assurance, Marvis, and location services

250 original practice questions for the Juniper Networks Certified Specialist - Mist AI Wireless
(JNCIS-MistAI-Wireless, exam JN0-452). The bank focuses on intermediate wireless decisions: interpreting RF and
802.11 behavior, deploying Mist APs and WLAN objects, applying MPSK and WxLAN policy, investigating wireless
security events, troubleshooting SLE classifiers, using Marvis evidence, and selecting the right location service.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

The JNCIS-MistAI-Wireless is designed for **wireless LAN engineers, campus network engineers, Mist implementation
consultants, WLAN architects, field engineers, and operations staff** who already understand the Mist platform and
need deeper skills in RF, WLAN configuration, wireless assurance, AI-assisted troubleshooting, and location services.

**Prerequisite:** JNCIA-MistAI.

**Typical study time:** 5-8 weeks of focused study, depending on hands-on WLAN and Juniper Mist experience.

## Juniper Mist AI Certification Path

The current Mist AI path starts with **JNCIA-MistAI (JN0-253)**. You can then take either
**JNCIS-MistAI-Wireless (JN0-452)** or **JNCIS-MistAI-Wired (JN0-460)**. Either specialist certification satisfies
the stated prerequisite path into **JNCIP-MistAI (JN0-750)**.

## What to Study First

1. **Build the connection-stage map** - discovery, association, security, authorization, DHCP, DNS, and application reachability are different failure layers.
2. **Apply RF fundamentals** - bands, SNR, MCS, channel width, contention, and roaming must lead to a real Mist AP/client decision.
3. **Separate WLAN access methods** - guest portal, MPSK, 802.1X, and WxLAN solve different authentication and policy needs.
4. **Troubleshoot through SLE evidence** - start with the failed SLE, classifier, affected clients/APs/WLANs, events, and packet evidence.
5. **Keep the Marvis surfaces distinct** - Natural Language, Query Language, Actions, and Minis have different jobs.
6. **Map the location services** - Wi-Fi location, vBLE, User Engagement, Asset Visibility, and Proximity Tracing are related but not interchangeable.
