## Who is this exam for?

The PL-900 is the entry-level certification for Microsoft Power Platform — the suite of low-code/no-code tools that lets anyone build business applications, automate processes, and create websites without writing traditional code. It's ideal for business users, IT professionals, and anyone who wants to understand what Power Apps, Power Automate, Power Pages, and Dataverse can do.

This exam was significantly updated in June 2025 — Power BI was removed as a standalone domain, Copilot and AI features were added throughout, and Power Pages now has a bigger role. If you're studying from older materials, make sure they cover the June 2025 objectives.

- [Describe the value of connectors to integrate services and data](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/introduction-power-platform/3-data-connectors)

### Manage the Microsoft Power Platform environment (15–20%)

This domain covers Dataverse (the data platform behind Power Platform) and how administrators manage environments, security, and governance. The June 2025 update added more Dataverse content and an administration sub-area that was previously in a different domain.

#### Describe Microsoft Dataverse

Dataverse is a cloud database that stores the data behind Power Apps and Power Automate. Unlike a traditional database, it includes built-in business logic, security, and integration with the rest of Power Platform. You should understand tables, columns, relationships, and how to import/export data.

- [Describe differences between traditional databases and Dataverse](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/introduction-common-data-service/2-overview)
- [Describe tables, columns, and relationships in Dataverse](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/introduction-common-data-service/3-identify-entities-fields)
- [Describe the options for business logic in Dataverse](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/introduction-common-data-service/4-business-rules)
- [Create and modify tables and columns through conversations](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-apps/maker/data-platform/create-edit-entities-portal)
- [Describe options for importing and exporting data](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-apps/developer/data-platform/import-data)

#### Describe Microsoft Power Platform administration and governance

- [Describe the Microsoft Power Platform security model](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-platform/admin/security/overview)
- [Describe environments](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-platform/admin/environments-overview)
- [Describe the different Microsoft Power Platform admin centers and portals](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-platform/admin/admin-documentation)
- [Describe how Microsoft Power Platform supports data privacy and accessibility guidelines](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-platform/admin/security/overview)

### Demonstrate the capabilities of Power Apps (25–30%)

This is the largest domain on the exam. Power Apps lets you build custom business applications without writing code. There are two main types: canvas apps (pixel-perfect design, connect to any data) and model-driven apps (data-first, automatically generated UI from Dataverse tables). You'll need to know when to use each and how to build basic apps.

#### Identify Power Apps capabilities

- [Describe use cases for and capabilities of canvas apps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/get-started-with-powerapps/2-powerapps-building-blocks)
- [Describe use cases for and capabilities of model-driven apps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/get-started-with-model-driven-apps-in-powerapps/2-building-blocks-of-model-driven-apps)
- [Explore model-driven app features](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/get-started-with-model-driven-apps-in-powerapps/3-explore-model-driven-apps)

#### Build a canvas app

Canvas apps give you full control over the layout — like designing a slide in PowerPoint. You can connect to hundreds of data sources using connectors, add controls (buttons, forms, galleries), and use Copilot to help build.

- [Connect to data sources by using connectors](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/build-app-solution/3-data-sources)
- [Describe common controls](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/build-app-solution/2-learn-basic-building-blocks)
- [Build responsive pages by using containers](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-apps/maker/canvas-apps/build-responsive-apps)
- [Describe the capabilities of Copilot controls](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-apps/maker/canvas-apps/ai-overview)
- [Share a canvas app](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/manage-apps-in-powerapps/2-share-app)

#### Build a model-driven app

Model-driven apps are built on top of Dataverse tables — the UI is automatically generated from your data model. They're ideal for complex business processes where data structure is more important than pixel-perfect design.

- [Create a model-driven app from tables](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/how-build-model-driven-app/2-building-blocks)
- [Modify forms](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/how-build-model-driven-app/3-create-model-driven-app)
- [Create and modify views](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/how-build-model-driven-app/3-create-model-driven-app)
- [Share a model-driven app](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/manage-apps-in-powerapps/2-share-app)

### Demonstrate the capabilities of Power Automate (15–20%)

Power Automate lets you automate repetitive tasks and business processes without writing code. There are two main types: cloud flows (run in the cloud, triggered by events or schedules) and desktop flows (automate tasks on your PC, like clicking through legacy apps). The June 2025 update added more content on desktop flows and Process Mining.

#### Identify Power Automate components

- [Describe use cases for cloud and desktop flows](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/get-started-flows/2-types-of-flows)
- [Describe use cases for cloud flow templates](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/get-started-flows/3-flow-templates)
- [Describe how Power Automate cloud flows use connector triggers and actions](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/get-started-flows/4-flow-components)
- [Describe Power Automate use cases for approvals, Microsoft Teams, Outlook, SharePoint, Forms, and document automation](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/get-started-flows/2-types-of-flows)
- [Describe the Power Automate apps including Power Automate for Desktop, Power Automate mobile, and the Power Automate maker portal](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-automate/getting-started)
- [Describe actions for Power Automate desktop flows](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-automate/desktop-flows/actions-reference)
- [Describe Process Mining](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-automate/process-mining-overview)
- [Describe loops and branching in cloud flows](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/build-more-flows/2-expand-with-conditions)

#### Build a Power Automate flow

### Demonstrate the capabilities of Power Pages (10–15%)

Power Pages (formerly Power Apps Portals) lets you create external-facing websites that connect to Dataverse data. It's the smallest domain on the exam but was expanded in the June 2025 update to include hands-on site creation with Copilot. Think of Power Pages as "SharePoint for external users."

- [Describe Power Pages security concepts](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-pages/security/power-pages-security)

#### Create a Power Pages site

- [Create and modify Power Pages sites by using Copilot](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-pages/getting-started/create-manage)
- [Describe design studio workspaces](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-pages/configure/design-studio)
