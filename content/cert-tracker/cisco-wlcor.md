## About the WLCOR Exam

> Implement and operate core Cisco wireless — RF, 802.11, deployment, client connectivity, monitoring, and automation & AI

Conquer the new CCNP Wireless core exam with 250 original practice questions covering RF fundamentals (radio-wave propagation, RF signal measurements including RSSI, transmit power, receive sensitivity, noise, and SNR, the principles of RF mathematics and EIRP, and Wi-Fi antenna characteristics), 802.11 technology fundamentals (Wi-Fi governance, usable channel and power combinations, modulation, channel width, MIMO/MU-MIMO/MLO, wireless topologies, and frame types), wireless network implementation (Cisco wireless architectures, physical infrastructure connections, and AP/WLC/dashboard management access), wireless network operation (Catalyst 9800 initial setup, the CAPWAP AP discovery and join process, AP modes, WLAN access, and client management across WLC, Meraki Dashboard, Catalyst Center, ISE, and Cisco Spaces), client connectivity configuration (authentication on and off the controller with WPA2/WPA3 and 802.1X/EAP, multi-OS supplicants, roaming, and guest networking), wireless monitoring and management (maintenance, client monitoring, troubleshooting client connectivity, and platform integrations), and automation and AI (Python, NETCONF/YANG, wireless infrastructure APIs, and Catalyst Center AI Analytics, AI Operations, and AI-RRM). Built to exam topics v1.0 (effective March 2026). Original practice questions.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

The WLCOR is designed for **experienced professionals validating core wireless skills**. It is the **core exam of the new standalone CCNP Wireless track** (pair it with a concentration such as 300-120 WLSI or 300-110 WLSD to earn CCNP Wireless), it satisfies the **CCIE Wireless** qualifying-exam requirement, and it independently earns the **Cisco Certified Specialist – Wireless Core** credential. 3-5 years of enterprise networking experience with a wireless focus is recommended.

**Typical study time:** 8-12 weeks of intensive study

## Study Tips

1. **Get fluent in RF math** — the rule of 3s and 10s (dB), converting mW to dBm, and EIRP (radio power minus cable loss plus antenna gain) show up throughout RF fundamentals and design
2. **Learn the Catalyst 9800 tag model** — WLAN profile plus policy profile bound by a policy tag, with site and RF tags, is the backbone of operation and client connectivity
3. **Know the CAPWAP join order** — DHCP option 43, DNS, subnet broadcast, and priming, plus primary/secondary/tertiary fallback, are high-yield
4. **Use our practice exam** — try the 20 free questions first to gauge your readiness
5. **Review explanations** — don't just check if you got it right; read why each answer is correct and why the alternatives fall short
6. **Simulate exam conditions** — use the timed exam mode to practice under pressure
