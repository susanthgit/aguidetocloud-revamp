## About the GH-200 Exam

> Master GitHub Actions — authoring and managing workflows, consuming and troubleshooting runs, building actions, enterprise runner and secret management, and securing automation with OIDC and least-privilege tokens.

The complete practice exam for GitHub Actions (GH-200). Covers authoring and managing workflows (events and triggers, jobs and conditional logic, matrix strategies, reusable workflows, caching and artifacts, environments and concurrency), consuming and troubleshooting runs (reading logs, diagnosing matrix failures, starter vs reusable workflows vs composite actions), authoring and maintaining actions (JavaScript, Docker, and composite actions, action metadata, Marketplace publishing, SHA pinning and versioning), managing GitHub Actions across the enterprise (allowed-actions policies, GitHub-hosted and self-hosted runners, runner groups, organization, repository, and environment secrets and variables), and securing and optimizing automation (environment approvals, GITHUB_TOKEN permissions, OIDC cloud federation, script-injection mitigation, artifact attestations) — every question a real-world scenario with full explanations.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

The GH-200 is designed for **developers, DevOps engineers, and platform engineers** who build and operate CI/CD with GitHub Actions. Hands-on experience writing workflows is recommended.

**Prerequisites:** None (working knowledge of GitHub recommended)

**Typical study time:** 4-8 weeks of focused study

## Exam Domains & Weights

The GH-200 exam covers **5 domains**. Focus your study time based on the weights below — higher-weighted domains have more exam questions.

| Domain | Weight | Practice Qs |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Author and manage workflows | 25% | 63 |
| Consume and troubleshoot workflows | 20% | 50 |
| Author and maintain actions | 20% | 50 |
| Manage GitHub Actions for the enterprise | 25% | 62 |
| Secure and optimize automation | 10% | 25 |
| **Total** | **100%** | **250** |

> 💡 **Study tip:** **Author and manage workflows** and **Manage GitHub Actions for the enterprise** carry the most weight (25% each) — start there. **Secure and optimize automation** has the least (10%), but don't skip it — exam questions can come from any domain, and the security topics (GITHUB_TOKEN permissions, OIDC, SHA pinning) show up everywhere.
