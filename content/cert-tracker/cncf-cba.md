## About the CBA Exam

> Prove you can build and run an internal developer portal on Backstage

Master Backstage end to end — the local development workflow (creating and running an app with @backstage/create-app and yarn dev, the packages/app + packages/backend monorepo, TypeScript, and building a container image), the framework and its React frontend / Node.js backend client-server architecture, configuration with app-config.yaml and deploying to production, the Software Catalog (entity kinds, catalog-info.yaml, annotations, locations, discovery, and troubleshooting ingestion), and customizing Backstage (frontend versus backend plugins, the backend system, wiring plugins, editing React code in the App, and Material UI theming). Original practice questions covering all 4 official CBA domains. Not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sourced from CNCF or Linux Foundation certification exams.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

The CBA is designed for **platform engineers, developers, DevOps engineers, and SREs** who are setting up, running, or customizing a Backstage internal developer portal (IDP). There are no formal prerequisites; a working familiarity with JavaScript/TypeScript, React, YAML, and containers is helpful but not required. The exam is multiple-choice and focuses on how Backstage works — its development workflow, architecture, the software catalog, and plugin-based customization.

**Typical study time:** 2-4 weeks of focused study

## Exam Domains & Weights

The CBA exam covers **4 domains**. Focus your study time based on the weights below — higher-weighted domains have more exam questions.

| Domain | Weight | Practice Qs |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Backstage Development Workflow | 24% | 60 |
| Backstage Infrastructure | 22% | 55 |
| Backstage Catalog | 22% | 55 |
| Customizing Backstage | 32% | 80 |
| **Total** | **100%** | **250** |

> 💡 **Study tip:** **Customizing Backstage** (32%) is the single heaviest domain — know the difference between frontend and backend plugins, how a plugin is wired into packages/app (routes, the EntityPage) and packages/backend (createBackend + backend.add), and how the UI is themed with Material UI and createUnifiedTheme. The **Software Catalog** (22%) is the next priority: know the entity kinds (Component, API, System, Domain, Resource, Group, User, Location) and how catalog-info.yaml, annotations, and discovery work together.
