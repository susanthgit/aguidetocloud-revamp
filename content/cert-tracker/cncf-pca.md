## About the PCA Exam

> Monitor cloud native systems with Prometheus and PromQL

Master open-source monitoring with Prometheus — observability concepts (metrics, logs and events, tracing, push vs pull, service discovery, and SLOs/SLIs/SLAs), Prometheus architecture and the pull model, scrape configuration, the data model and exposition format, PromQL (selectors, rate/increase, aggregation over time and dimensions, binary operators, and histogram_quantile), instrumentation with client libraries, exporters like node_exporter and blackbox_exporter, metric naming conventions, recording and alerting rules, Alertmanager routing and grouping, and dashboarding with Grafana. Original practice questions covering all 5 official PCA domains. Not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sourced from CNCF or Linux Foundation certification exams.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

The PCA is designed for **SREs, platform engineers, and observability engineers** who run Prometheus in production. There are no formal prerequisites; hands-on experience with Prometheus and PromQL is recommended.

**Typical study time:** 3-6 weeks of focused study

The PCA exam covers **5 domains**. Focus your study time based on the weights below — higher-weighted domains have more exam questions.

| Domain | Weight | Practice Qs |
