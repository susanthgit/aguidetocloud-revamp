## About the DCID Exam

> Design a modern Cisco data center for both traditional and AI/ML workloads — network, compute, storage, and automation

The 300-610 DCID (Designing Cisco Data Center Infrastructure, exam topics **v1.2**) is a **CCNP concentration exam** — pair it with the DCCOR 350-601 core for **CCNP Data Center**. Passing also earns the **Cisco Certified Specialist - Data Center Design** credential. The v1.2 update reshaped the exam for **traditional and AI/ML workloads**. It validates your ability to design a Cisco data center across four areas: **network design** (AI/ML workload profiles — training versus inference, GPUs/DPUs/SmartNICs; high-performance and AI networking with **RoCEv2** on lossless Ethernet using **PFC/ECN/DCQCN**, plus **InfiniBand** and **RDMA** as design alternatives; Layer 2 with **vPC** and LACP; Layer 3 with graceful restart, NSF, and **VRF-lite**; QoS for lossless Ethernet; Data Center Interconnect with **VXLAN BGP EVPN**; management and redundancy with **Cisco Nexus Dashboard**; and segmentation with VXLAN and Cisco ACI); **compute design** (Cisco **UCS** B/C/X-Series, **Fabric Interconnect** switch mode versus end-host mode, oversubscription, **VIC** adapters and service profiles, UCS **X-Series**, and compute for AI/ML); **storage network design** (**iSCSI** with MPIO multipathing, **Fibre Channel** port types and oversubscription, VSAN and zoning, **FCoE**, and storage deployment options); and **automation design** (**Cisco Intersight**, **NX-API**, model-driven programmability, Ansible, Python, and Terraform; service-profile, vNIC, and vHBA templates; **Intersight Cloud Orchestrator**; and Nexus Dashboard automation). Original practice questions. Not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sourced from Cisco Systems certification exams.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

DCID is designed for **data center design engineers, network and compute architects, and solutions architects who design Cisco data center infrastructure** built on Nexus switches, Cisco UCS, MDS SAN, Cisco Intersight, and Nexus Dashboard — increasingly for **AI/ML workloads**. It suits candidates pursuing CCNP Data Center. Cisco recommends 3-5 years of data center design experience across network, compute, storage, and automation. You should be comfortable **evaluating design options and tradeoffs**: choosing RoCEv2 Ethernet versus InfiniBand for a GPU training cluster, selecting a Fabric Interconnect operation mode for an upstream-LAN constraint, sizing Fibre Channel oversubscription, planning iSCSI multipathing and isolation, choosing VXLAN EVPN versus ACI for multi-tenancy, and picking the right automation approach (Intersight, NX-API, Ansible, Terraform, or Nexus Dashboard) for a requirement.

**Typical study time:** 6-10 weeks of focused study

## Cisco Certification Path

DCID is a **concentration** exam. For **CCNP Data Center**, pass the **DCCOR 350-601** core plus one concentration — **300-610 DCID** (data center design) is one of four concentration choices alongside DCACI 300-620, DCIT 300-615, and DCNAUTO 300-635. Cisco's track runs: Associate (CCNA) → Professional (CCNP) → Expert (CCIE).

## Study Tips

1. **Start with Network Design** — at 35% it is the largest slice, and the AI/ML networking topics (RoCEv2 vs InfiniBand, lossless Ethernet, non-blocking fabrics) are the newest and most heavily emphasized in v1.2
2. **Think like a designer, not an operator** — DCID tests design **decisions and tradeoffs** (which option fits this constraint and why), not CLI syntax
3. **Master the Fabric Interconnect modes** — Ethernet end-host mode vs switch mode, and FC end-host (NPV) vs FC switch mode, are classic Compute Design traps
4. **Know your Fibre Channel port types cold** — N, F, E, TE, and NP ports, plus ISL and oversubscription math, drive most Storage Network Design questions
5. **Use our practice exam** — try the 20 free questions first to gauge your readiness
6. **Match the automation tool to the job** — Intersight, NX-API, model-driven programmability, Ansible, Python, Terraform, and Nexus Dashboard each fit different requirements
7. **Simulate exam conditions** — use the timed exam mode to practice under pressure
