## About the MB-820 Exam

> Build AL extensions for Dynamics 365 Business Central - objects, events, the AL language, testing, telemetry, and REST/API integration (Microsoft's developer cert MB-820).

Master Exam MB-820 (Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central Developer) - describe the Business Central architecture, the extension model, and online vs on-premises; install and configure the VS Code AL development environment (app.json, launch.json, symbols, and the CodeCop/PerTenantExtensionCop/AppSourceCop/UICop analyzers) and debug, deploy, and maintain extensions; develop with AL objects (tables and FlowFields, pages and page types, Role Centers, reports, XMLports, codeunits, integration and business events, interfaces, install and upgrade codeunits, permission sets and entitlements, and queries); develop with the AL language (data types, procedures, error handling with TryFunctions and collectible errors, access modifiers, and the user-assistance and design-pattern standards); work with development tools (the Test Toolkit, test codeunits and handler functions, and Application Insights telemetry via Session.LogMessage); and integrate Business Central with other applications using HttpClient and JSON, API pages and queries, OData bound and unbound actions, and Read Scale-Out.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

The MB-820 is designed for **Business Central developers** who build and extend apps with the AL language in Visual Studio Code - ISV/AppSource partners, per-tenant-extension (PTE) developers, and consultant-developers. You should have hands-on AL experience (objects, events, the development environment) plus familiarity with AppSource, source control, and CI/CD. Around 1-2 years of AL/Business Central development is recommended; it is the developer counterpart to the MB-800 functional consultant cert.

**Typical study time:** 4-8 weeks of focused study
