## About the ENSLD Exam

> Design advanced enterprise networks — addressing and routing, campus, WAN, services, and automation

Conquer the CCNP Enterprise ENSLD concentration with 250 original practice questions covering advanced addressing and routing design (structured IPv4/IPv6 addressing plans, and stable, secure, and scalable IS-IS, EIGRP, OSPF, and BGP designs, plus IPv6 migration strategies), advanced enterprise campus networks (high availability with FHRP, StackWise/VSS, graceful restart, and BFD; Layer 2 with STP scalability, loop-free technologies, and Layer 2 security; multicampus Layer 3; and SD-Access architecture and fabric design), WAN for enterprise networks (MPLS Layer 3 VPN, Metro Ethernet, and DWDM connectivity; DMVPN, IPsec, and GET VPN site-to-site design; WAN high availability; and Cisco SD-WAN architecture and design), network services (DiffServ and IntServ QoS strategy, end-to-end QoS policy, network management design, and multicast with PIM, SSM, and MSDP), and automation and artificial intelligence (IETF/OpenConfig/Cisco YANG, NETCONF/RESTCONF, model-driven telemetry, gRPC/gNMI, and cloud connectivity and service models). ## Who Should Take This Exam?

The ENSLD is designed for **experienced professionals seeking network design validation**. It is a CCNP Enterprise concentration (paired with the ENCOR 350-401 core) and also earns the standalone **Cisco Certified Specialist – Enterprise Design** credential. 3-5 years of enterprise networking experience, with a design focus, is recommended.

**Typical study time:** 8-12 weeks of intensive study

## Study Tips

1. **Think like a designer, not an operator** — ENSLD tests design decisions and trade-offs; for each option ask which one meets the stated scale, availability, and security constraints, not which command to type
2. **Master addressing for summarization** — clean hierarchical IPv4/IPv6 addressing is the foundation for scalable routing design across the whole exam
3. **Use our practice exam** — try the 20 free questions first to gauge your readiness
4. **Review explanations** — don't just check if you got it right; read why each design is correct and why the alternatives fall short
5. **Simulate exam conditions** — use the timed exam mode to practice under pressure
