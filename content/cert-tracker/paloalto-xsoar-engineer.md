Every question includes a real-world SOAR-engineering scenario, detailed explanations, why-wrong analysis, and exam tips — spanning engine deployment and content lifecycle, Marketplace pack and integration-instance configuration, classifiers and mappers, incident types, layouts and SLAs, playbook task types and context-data manipulation, filters and transformers, custom automation scripts and jobs, the War Room, and indicator enrichment, source reliability, and sharing.

## Who Should Take This Exam?

The XSOAR-Engineer is designed for **security automation engineers, SOAR content developers, and SOC engineers** who build and maintain Cortex XSOAR. It validates the skills to deploy and configure the platform (engines, authentication, dev/prod content lifecycle, Marketplace packs, integration instances), engineer the incident lifecycle (classifiers, mappers, incident types, fields, layouts, SLAs, pre/post-processing), develop playbooks and automation scripts, and manage threat intelligence. (It is distinct from the XSIAM exams: XSIAM Analyst *uses* the platform as a SOC analyst, and XSIAM Engineer builds the XSIAM SIEM/XDR platform — this exam is about the standalone XSOAR product.)

**Typical study time:** 5-8 weeks alongside hands-on Cortex XSOAR content-development experience

## Exam Domains & Weights

The XSOAR-Engineer exam covers **5 domains**. Focus your study time based on the weights below — higher-weighted domains have more exam questions.

| Domain | Weight | Practice Qs |

| **Total** | **100%** | **250** |

> 💡 **Study tip:** **Playbook Development** (30%) is by far the heaviest domain — master task inputs/outputs and results, the task types (conditional, data-collection, manual, section), sub-playbook inputs/outputs and looping, context-data manipulation with the transform language, the difference between filters and transformers, the playbook debugger, and writing custom automation scripts. **Use Case Planning and Development** (22%) is next: know cold the difference between a classifier (decides the incident *type*) and a mapper (maps *fields*), plus pre-processing rules versus post-processing scripts. Don't underweight **Threat Intelligence Management** (18%) — indicator creation methods, source reliability grading, and exclusion lists show up throughout.

## Study Tips

1. **Live in the playbook editor** — Playbook Development is 30% of the exam; build real playbooks with conditional tasks, sub-playbooks, loops, filters, and transformers until the mechanics are second nature
2. **Nail classifier vs mapper** — a classifier decides the incident *type*; a mapper maps *fields* in and out. Confusing the two is the most common exam trap
3. **Use our practice exam** — try the 20 free questions first to gauge your readiness
4. **Review explanations** — don't just check if you got it right; read why each answer is correct
5. **Know the content lifecycle** — Marketplace pack dependencies, dev/prod remote repositories, and integration-instance troubleshooting underpin the Planning and Maintenance domain
6. **Simulate exam conditions** — use the timed exam mode to practice under pressure
