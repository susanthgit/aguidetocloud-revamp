## What Is Microsoft 365 Copilot?

Microsoft 365 Copilot is an **AI-powered assistant** built directly into the Microsoft 365 apps you already use every day. It combines the power of large language models (like GPT-4) with **your organisation's data** in Microsoft Graph — your emails, files, meetings, chats, and calendar.

Think of it as having a smart colleague who has read every document in your company and can instantly help you write, analyse, summarise, and create.

### How Does It Work?

```
Your Prompt  →  Microsoft 365 Copilot  →  Output
   ↑                    ↑                    ↓
 You type          Combines LLM +       Draft, summary,
 what you need     your M365 data       chart, email, etc.
```

Copilot follows a simple loop: you give it a **prompt** (a natural language instruction), it looks at **your data** (emails, files, meetings), and generates a **useful output** — a document draft, a data summary, meeting notes, or whatever you asked for.

> Microsoft 365 Copilot respects your existing security permissions — it can only access data you already have access to. [Learn more about Copilot data security](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/copilot/microsoft-365/microsoft-365-copilot-privacy).

## Copilot in Word

Copilot in Word helps you **draft, rewrite, and transform** documents using natural language prompts.

### What Can You Do?

| Feature | Example Prompt |
|---------|---------------|
| **Draft a document** | "Write a project proposal for migrating our email to Microsoft 365" |
| **Rewrite text** | "Make this paragraph more professional and concise" |
| **Summarise** | "Summarise this 10-page document in 5 bullet points" |
| **Change tone** | "Rewrite this email to sound more friendly" |
| **Add content** | "Add a section about security considerations" |

Copilot in Word can reference **other files** in your OneDrive or SharePoint — so you can say "Draft a summary based on /Sales Report Q1.xlsx" and it will pull data from that file.

📖 [Official guide: Use Copilot in Word](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/copilot-word) · [Copilot in Word documentation](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/copilot/microsoft-365/copilot-word)

## Copilot in Excel

Copilot in Excel turns your data into **insights, formulas, and visualisations** — all through natural language.

### What Can You Do?

| Feature | Example Prompt |
|---------|---------------|
| **Analyse data** | "What are the top 5 products by revenue?" |
| **Create charts** | "Create a bar chart showing sales by region" |
| **Generate formulas** | "Add a column that calculates profit margin" |
| **Highlight patterns** | "Highlight rows where sales dropped more than 20%" |
| **Ask questions** | "Is there a correlation between marketing spend and revenue?" |

> **Important:** For Copilot in Excel to work, your data must be in an [Excel Table](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/office/overview-of-excel-tables-7ab0bb7d-3a9e-4b56-a3c9-6c94334e492c) format (not just a range). Select your data and press **Ctrl+T** to convert it.

📖 [Official guide: Use Copilot in Excel](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/copilot-excel) · [Copilot in Excel documentation](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/copilot/microsoft-365/copilot-excel)

## Copilot in Teams

Copilot in Teams is a game-changer for **meetings, chat, and collaboration**. It can summarise what you missed, extract action items, and help you catch up instantly.

### During Meetings

- **Summarise the meeting so far** — "What have we discussed?"
- **List action items** — "What tasks were assigned and to whom?"
- **Catch up if you joined late** — "What did I miss in the last 10 minutes?"
- **Get opinions** — "What were the different viewpoints on the budget?"

### In Chat

- **Summarise long threads** — "Summarise the key points from the last 7 days"
- **Find decisions** — "What decisions were made this week?"
- **Draft replies** — "Write a reply agreeing with the proposal"

> **Note:** Meeting transcription must be enabled for Copilot to work in meetings. Your IT admin can enable this in the Teams admin centre.

📖 [Official guide: Use Copilot in Teams](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/copilot-teams) · [Copilot in Teams documentation](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/copilot/microsoft-365/copilot-teams)

## Business Chat (BizChat)

Business Chat — also known as **Microsoft 365 Chat** or **BizChat** — is Copilot's cross-app experience. It can search across **all your Microsoft 365 data** (emails, files, chats, meetings, contacts) to answer questions and generate content.

### Example Prompts

- "What did my team discuss about the budget this week?"
- "Summarise all emails from Sarah about the Azure migration"
- "What files were shared in the Marketing channel this month?"
- "Prepare a briefing for my meeting with the CTO tomorrow"

Access BizChat at [microsoft365.com/chat](https://microsoft365.com/chat) or in the Microsoft 365 app.

📖 [Microsoft 365 Copilot Business Chat](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/copilot/microsoft-365/microsoft-365-copilot-overview#microsoft-365-copilot-chat)

## Licensing and Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **License** | Microsoft 365 Copilot add-on ($30/user/month) on top of M365 E3/E5, Business Standard/Premium |
| **Prerequisite** | Microsoft 365 E3, E5, Business Standard, or Business Premium |
| **Data residency** | Follows your Microsoft 365 tenant data boundary |
| **Admin setup** | Enable via Microsoft 365 admin centre — [Setup guide](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/copilot/microsoft-365/microsoft-365-copilot-setup) |

> **Free alternative:** [Microsoft Copilot](https://copilot.microsoft.com) (formerly Bing Chat) is free for everyone — but it doesn't connect to your organisation's Microsoft 365 data.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a special licence for Microsoft 365 Copilot?**
Yes — you need the Microsoft 365 Copilot add-on licence ($30/user/month) on top of a qualifying Microsoft 365 plan (E3, E5, Business Standard, or Business Premium).

**Can Copilot access all my company's data?**
No — Copilot can only access data that **you** already have permission to see. It follows your existing Microsoft 365 security and access controls.

**Does Copilot work offline?**
No — Copilot requires an internet connection and a valid Microsoft 365 licence to function.

**What's the difference between Microsoft Copilot and Microsoft 365 Copilot?**
Microsoft Copilot (free, at copilot.microsoft.com) is a general AI assistant. Microsoft 365 Copilot ($30/month) is the enterprise version that connects to your organisation's emails, files, meetings, and chats.

**Can I use Copilot in the browser version of Office?**
Yes — Copilot works in both the desktop apps and the web versions of Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Outlook.
