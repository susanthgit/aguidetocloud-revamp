How to control your Cowork burn

  • Set hard caps and alerts in M365 admin center → Copilot → Cost Management. Caps stop spend before it happens; alerts tell you when thresholds hit.
  • Scope to groups, not "All users" in your spending policy. Start with a pilot cohort, measure their patterns for 30 days, then decide whether to expand.
  • Check the Consumption tab weekly — usage by user, group, service. Find the heavy users and ask them what they're getting for the spend.
  • Teach the difference between Copilot (included) and Cowork (metered) — most everyday tasks already work in the $30 seat via Researcher, Analyst, and Office agents. Cowork is for multi-tool orchestration.
  • Use any transition runway before billing starts — if you joined through the Frontier preview, check your tenant's billing start date in the admin center and use the runway to set caps and train users.

How this calculator works

The model: we multiply your number of users by a typical credits-per-user-per-month figure for the usage level you pick — Light, Balanced, or Heavy — then convert credits to dollars at $0.01 each (PayGo).

Where the usage levels come from (Microsoft's GA credit guidance — light ~20, medium ~100, heavy ~400 credits per prompt, across a typical monthly prompt count):

  • Light ≈ 1,150–2,220 credits / user / month — occasional, mostly quick tasks.
  • Balanced ≈ 3,690–7,080 credits / user / month — a typical mix.
  • Heavy ≈ 9,600–18,200 credits / user / month — frequent, multi-tool work.

The low–high band reflects the real spread in per-task credit cost. Different teams differ: if your light users and power users are very different, run the calculator twice and add the results.

Dollars and credits: Copilot Credits are what the admin Cost Management dashboard meters; dollars are credits × $0.01 (PayGo). Prepaid capacity packs are effectively cheaper per credit. Currencies other than USD are an approximate conversion for budgeting — Microsoft bills Cowork in USD.

Sources: Microsoft 365 Blog — Copilot Cowork GA · Usage-based billing overview (Microsoft Learn) · for a detailed per-persona model, ask your Microsoft account team about the Customer Cowork Estimator.
