What do Light / Medium / Heavy mean?

Light (~20 credits / prompt): quick briefs from a few files, simple coordination, a tidy-up of your week.

Medium (~100 credits / prompt): multi-step workflows, a research memo grounded in your M365 data, prep across two or three sources.

Heavy (~400 credits / prompt): long-running, multi-tool orchestration with lots of context — research + draft + deck in one go.

These are typical mid-points. Real per-task credits range widely (light 5–50, medium 50–200, heavy 200–1000+) depending on the model picked, tools called, and context size. The range band in Step 3 reflects that.

How to control your Cowork burn

  • Set hard caps and alerts in M365 admin center → Copilot → Cost Management. Caps stop spend before it happens; alerts tell you when thresholds hit.
  • Scope to groups, not "All users" in your spending policy. Start with a pilot cohort, measure their patterns for 30 days, then decide whether to expand.
  • Check the Consumption tab weekly — usage by user, group, service. Find the heavy users and ask them what they're getting for the spend.
  • Teach the difference between Copilot (included) and Cowork (metered) — most everyday tasks already work in the $30 seat via Researcher, Analyst, and Office agents. Cowork is for multi-tool orchestration.
  • Use any transition runway before billing starts — if you joined through the Frontier preview, check your tenant's billing start date in the admin center and use the runway to set caps, test patterns, and train users before metered spend begins.
{{/* ── Methodology ── */}}

How this calculator works

The model: for each of the four worker groups, you set how many people use Cowork and how their tasks split across light, medium, and heavy. We multiply users by your prompts-per-day × working-days assumption to get monthly prompts, then apply a typical credit cost per task type and sum across all groups.

Credit estimates (typical mid-points from Microsoft's GA guidance):

  • Light ~20 credits / prompt (full range 5–50)
  • Medium ~100 credits / prompt (full range 50–200)
  • Heavy ~400 credits / prompt (full range 200–1000+)

The low–high band in Step 3 uses a typical spread around those mid-points (roughly light 15–30, medium 75–150, heavy 300–550) so the range stays credible rather than swinging across the full extremes.

Dollars and credits: we show both because they're the same thing at two layers — Copilot Credits are what the admin Cost Management dashboard meters, and dollars are credits × $0.01 (PayGo). Prepaid capacity packs are effectively cheaper per credit. Microsoft's admin tools and prepaid packs speak in credits; your finance team speaks in dollars — so we surface both.

Caveats: this is a directional model, not a billing forecast. Real burn depends on which models users pick, the tools each task calls, context size, and runtime. Microsoft expects per-task cost to fall over time as models get cheaper and Cowork gets better at matching the model to the task.

Sources: Microsoft 365 Blog — Copilot Cowork GA · Usage-based billing overview (Microsoft Learn) · for the official Microsoft persona estimator, ask your Microsoft account team about the Customer Cowork Estimator.
