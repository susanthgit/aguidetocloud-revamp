Where should the work go?

Default · already in your seat

Stay in M365 Copilot

"Research it, summarise it, turn it into a doc / workbook / deck, let me edit" is already in the $30 seat via Researcher, Analyst, and the Office agents. No meter for these.

When "help me" becomes "go do it"

Reach for Cowork

Worth it when you genuinely value unattended runtime, multi-tool coordination, and long follow-through across M365. Turn it on deliberately — with tenant / group / user caps, alerts, and a budget.

When you want predictable budget

Weigh the alternatives

Quota-based tools (e.g. Claude Pro / Team) bundle usage into a flat monthly fee — simple to budget, though you don't see a per-task cost. Microsoft reports Cowork ran 30–40% cheaper than the Claude Cowork alternative in its own M365-connected tests.

How to keep Cowork spend in check

  • Set hard caps and alerts in M365 admin center → Copilot → Cost Management. Caps stop spend before it happens; alerts tell you when thresholds hit.
  • Scope to groups, not "All users" in your spending policy. Start with a pilot cohort, measure their patterns for 30 days, then decide whether to expand.
  • Check the Consumption tab weekly — usage by user, group, service. Find the heavy users and see what they're getting for the spend.
  • Teach the line between Copilot (included) and Cowork (metered) — most everyday tasks already work in the $30 seat. Cowork is for unattended multi-tool orchestration.
  • Consider prepaid capacity packs — at scale, prepaid credits ($0.008 vs $0.01 PayGo) are effectively cheaper than pay-as-you-go.

How this calculator works

The model: we multiply your number of users by a typical credits-per-user-per-month figure for the usage level you pick, then convert credits to dollars at $0.01 each (PayGo). The result is shown as a low–high range.

Calibration: the levels are anchored to Microsoft's own Customer Cowork Estimator, whose default persona mixes are roughly $82–228 per user per month in credits on top of the seat:

  • Light ≈ $70–100 / user / month — lower-volume manager / senior leader style mix.
  • Medium ≈ $143–150 / user / month — corporate or customer-facing knowledge worker mix.
  • Heavy ≈ $200–260 / user / month — technical or power-user mix with more heavy prompts.

Dollars and credits: Copilot Credits are what the admin Cost Management dashboard meters; dollars are credits × $0.01 (PayGo). Prepaid capacity packs are effectively cheaper. Currencies other than USD are an approximate conversion for budgeting — Microsoft bills Cowork in USD.

Caveats: a directional model, not a billing forecast — Microsoft's estimator defaults are aggregated Frontier data and "may not predict future outcomes." Real burn depends on the model picked, tools called, context, and runtime; the cheaper "Cowork 1" model is coming. Use this for budgeting, not purchasing.

Sources: Microsoft 365 Blog — Copilot Cowork GA · Usage-based billing & Copilot Credits (Microsoft Learn) · your Microsoft account team can share the Customer Cowork Estimator workbook.
